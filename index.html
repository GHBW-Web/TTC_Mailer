As we enter a new year, much has been said and written about 2020 – much, that is focused on how best to forget a year like it; since, it brought tremendous hardship to the global communities, causing millions to suffer, as well as cause economic havoc across all economies. We continue to deal with the sufferings and there’s light at the end of the tunnel, thanks to modern medicine and vaccinations. While we are still not out of the woods, enterprises have been laser focused on working through their priorities and have started to look beyond, with a view to leveraging the crisis led opportunities for transformation.

As Lenin once said, sometimes nothing happens for decades, and sometimes decades happen in weeks – that is about the most apt description for the fallout of the pandemic driven crisis with enterprises across the world. Human cost notwithstanding, several outcomes of the crisis on businesses were a culmination of a few underlying trends that already existed in the environment – from smartphone driven experiences, ever expanding computing infrastructure with every device now being a computer, and many of them not mini, sensors, connected devices and so on.

We are both lifelong learners and have tried to capture in our book ‘Transformation in Times of Crisis’, winning strategy by using the eight principles, to transform any crisis into opportunities.

Ask Yourself: Are you courageous to change your business and revenue models to thrive during crisis and emerge as a disruptor?

Kodak was afraid of losing its film- based business and resisted venturing into digital photography. It lost its opportunity to become a leader in digital photography, though it had invented the technology. It is important to underline that you need to change your mental models and speed up transformation to pave a path of opportunities even during the crisis.
